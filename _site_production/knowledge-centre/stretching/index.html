General Guidelines for Stretching

13 Jan 2014

Stretching your muscles can be very benificial and a great way to incurage the results your massage therapist worked so hard for. But you need to be careful not to injure yourself while stretching.

Follow these 5 principles of stretching to reduce the risks of injury.

  • Stretch only to the point where you feel tightness or resistance to the stretch or perhaps some discomfort. Stretching should never be painful.
  • Always stretch slowly and with control.
  • Hold stretches for at least 30 seconds, and repeat each stretch 3 times.
  • Be sure to breathe normally during a stretch. Do not hold your breath.
  • Be sure to warm your muscles before stretching. You can warm your muscles by exercising lightly, by taking a warm bath or shower, or by applying heat.

Stretch Your Upper Trapezius Muscle

13 Jan 2014

To stretch your Upper Trapezius Muscle,begin in a seated position.With your shoulders relaxed and your head in a natural position, carefully bend your neck to bring your right ear toward your right armpit. If more pressure is needed to feel the stretch, you can use your right hand to gently pull from the side of your head just above your left ear toward your right armpit.
